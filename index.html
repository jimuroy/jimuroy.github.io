About Me

I am an MBA candidate at the University Canada West and an Instructor in Dental Office Administration at Granville College in Vancouver. With over a decade of experience spanning dental practice, clinic operations, and professional education, I specialize in bridging healthcare expertise with operational and administrative excellence.

My professional journey began in dentistry, where I developed a strong understanding of clinical workflows, patient care coordination, and healthcare service delivery. Over time, I expanded my expertise into education and training, where I now prepare students to become job-ready professionals for dental clinics across British Columbia. My teaching focuses on practical skills such as dental office management, insurance and billing systems, clinic software applications, and effective patient communication.

My Projects

LLM Query Based Research

This project examined the performance of large language models through a structured query-based evaluation design. Using a sample of 10 carefully designed prompts, the study generated 40 responses from each model to assess consistency, relevance, clarity, and practical usefulness. The objective was to compare how different LLMs respond to identical business-oriented queries and to identify strengths and weaknesses in accuracy, reasoning quality, and response reliability. The project involved prompt design, response collection, comparative analysis, and interpretation of findings to support evidence-based conclusions about LLM effectiveness in managerial and academic contexts. This work strengthened my skills in AI evaluation, analytical thinking, structured experimentation, and technology-driven decision making.
